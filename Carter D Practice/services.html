Photodynamic Therapy

Photodynamic Therapy (PDT) is a cutting-edge, non-invasive treatment that harnesses the power of light and a photosensitizing medication to selectively target abnormal or damaged skin cells. During the procedure, a special solution is applied to the skin, which is then activated by a specific wavelength of light. This process destroys precancerous cells (such as actinic keratoses), certain superficial skin cancers, and even stubborn acne lesions, while leaving healthy tissue largely unaffected. PDT is especially valued for its ability to treat large or multiple areas with minimal discomfort and downtime compared to traditional surgical methods.

Patients often choose PDT for its versatility and cosmetic benefits. In addition to treating precancerous and cancerous lesions, PDT can improve the appearance of sun-damaged skin, reduce oil production, and minimize pores. The treatment is performed in-office and typically requires little recovery time, with most patients experiencing only mild redness or peeling. Our team will guide you through every step, ensuring your comfort and safety while helping you achieve healthier, clearer skin.

  • Equipment Used:
  • Photosensitizing agent (e.g., Levulan Kerastick)
  • Blue light (BLU-U) photodynamic therapy lamp
  • Protective eyewear
  • Gentle skin cleanser

MOHS Micrographic Surgery

MOHS Micrographic Surgery is the gold standard for treating many types of skin cancer, particularly those located on the face, ears, and other cosmetically or functionally important areas. This highly specialized procedure involves removing thin layers of cancerous tissue one at a time, with each layer immediately examined under a microscope. The process is repeated until no cancer cells remain, ensuring complete removal while sparing as much healthy tissue as possible. This meticulous approach results in the highest cure rates—often exceeding 99% for certain skin cancers—while minimizing scarring and preserving appearance and function.

Our practice is committed to providing a comfortable, patient-centered experience throughout the MOHS process. The procedure is performed under local anesthesia in a safe, outpatient setting, and most patients can return home the same day. We understand that a skin cancer diagnosis can be stressful, so we prioritize clear communication, compassionate care, and expert reconstruction when needed. Our goal is to help you achieve the best possible medical and cosmetic outcome, so you can move forward with confidence and peace of mind.

  • Equipment Used:
  • Surgical instruments (scalpel, forceps, curettes)
  • Local anesthetic (e.g., lidocaine)
  • Microscope for tissue analysis
  • Sutures and wound care supplies
  • Sterile drapes and gloves
